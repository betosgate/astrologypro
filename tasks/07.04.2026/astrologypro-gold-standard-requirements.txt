# AstrologyPro Platform — Gold Standard Requirement Analysis

## 1. Document Purpose

This document converts the current task board and business notes into a build-ready product requirement for the AstrologyPro platform. It defines the public experience, diviner experience, customer experience, affiliate flow, monetization logic, operational rules, and system-level requirements.

This is not a light feature summary. It is intended to be used by product, design, engineering, QA, and operations teams as the baseline implementation document.

---

## 2. Product Vision

AstrologyPro is a marketplace + branded storefront + live engagement platform for diviners.

Each diviner gets a branded public page at `astrologypro.com/[username]` that acts as:

* their public profile
* their live-stream hub
* their product and service storefront
* a lead capture point
* an affiliate-driven conversion surface
* an entry point into a customer back office for purchased readings

The platform also includes:

* a main public marketing website
* a browse/search directory for diviners
* a diviner back office
* an admin back office
* a customer portal
* an affiliate system
* automated marketing based on check-ins and astrology event timing
* a recurring subscription product for weekly personalized updates
* a video reading system with optional phone dial-in and usage-based billing

---

## 3. Business Goals

1. Let each diviner run a branded business inside the platform without needing a separate website.
2. Convert live-stream viewers into leads, customers, subscribers, and returning clients.
3. Sell both time-based and non-time-based astrology/tarot products.
4. Build recurring revenue via monthly customer subscriptions and diviner SaaS plans.
5. Support affiliate-led sales with accurate attribution and payout tracking.
6. Give admins full operational control over diviners, products, commissions, subscriptions, and content.
7. Keep the user journey simple: discover diviner → watch live or browse → buy product → submit only required intake data → receive service → return via customer portal.

---

## 4. Core Product Principles

1. **Diviner-first storefront model**
   The diviner page is the core selling surface, not just a profile page.

2. **Payment before detailed intake**
   Users must pay before being asked for detailed birth or reading information, except for free check-in flows.

3. **Dynamic data collection**
   The platform must ask only for information that is required for the selected product.

4. **Strong ownership and attribution**
   The system must always know which diviner owns the customer journey and which affiliate, if any, drove the sale.

5. **Configurable monetization**
   Revenue splits, affiliate percentages, diviner subscriptions, and telephony pass-through costs must be configurable by business rules.

6. **Operational clarity**
   Admins and diviners must be able to see products, leads, purchases, commissions, payouts, subscriptions, reminders, and service fulfillment status clearly.

---

## 5. Assumptions and Governing Rules

The following assumptions are derived from the current notes and should be treated as baseline requirements unless business decides otherwise:

1. Each diviner has a unique public username and route.
2. The default public profile route is `astrologypro.com/[username]`.
3. The main public tabs on the diviner page are **Home** and **Bio**.
4. A secondary content area on the page must support **My Media** and **Testimonials**.
5. The **Check-In** feature is visible only when the diviner is currently live.
6. Products are divided into **Time-Based Products** and **Non-Time-Based Products**.
7. **Nativity/Birth Chart** must be visually emphasized as a key offering.
8. Customers must pay before entering detailed birth data for a purchased reading.
9. The weekly personalized update subscription is a recurring paid product, branded to the diviner.
10. A platform/diviner default revenue split exists, currently referenced as 70/30, but the system should allow configurable rules.
11. Affiliate commission is deducted from the diviner side unless business rules specify otherwise.
12. Affiliates may have unique product-specific links.
13. Diviners are responsible for paying affiliates; the system tracks the amount owed and payout status.
14. Diviners have a base SaaS plan and optional paid add-ons.
15. Phone dial-in usage for video readings is billed as pass-through usage to the diviner.
16. The platform must support both admin-managed and diviner-managed setup for affiliates.

## 6. User Roles

### 6.1 Visitor
Unauthenticated public user browsing the main site or a diviner page.

### 6.2 Customer
User who can purchase products, submit required intake details, manage subscriptions, and access prior readings or purchased services.

### 6.3 Diviner
Service provider with a public profile, product catalog, live stream presence, customers, affiliate relationships, content/media, and back-office tools.

### 6.4 Affiliate
User or partner who can sign up, receive tracked links, refer traffic and sales, and accumulate commissions tied to approved rules.

### 6.5 Admin
Platform operator with full control over diviners, users, products, subscriptions, revenue rules, compliance text, content moderation, and system configuration.

### 6.6 Support / Finance Operations (optional sub-role)
Back-office operational users who may need limited admin functions such as viewing orders, updating payout status, or moderating testimonials.

## 9. Detailed Functional Requirements

# 9A. Public Diviner Page (`/[username]`)

This is the most important user-facing surface in the platform.

## 9A.1 Routing and Identity
1. Each diviner must have a unique username/slug.
2. Username must be validated for uniqueness and reserved words.
3. Username changes must be restricted and audited to avoid broken links.
4. The public page must be SEO-friendly with indexable metadata.
5. The page must support direct linking from social profiles and affiliate links.

## 9A.2 Page Structure
The public diviner page must include:
* hero section
* profile image or brand image
* diviner name and title/tagline
* live status badge
* primary CTA area
* Home tab
* Bio tab
* My Media / Testimonials secondary area
* product listing area
* customer login link
* optional affiliate / promo content blocks

## 9A.3 Home Tab Requirements
The Home tab is the main commercial and engagement view. It must support:
1. Live stream area
2. Product/service listing area
3. Check-In feature when live
4. Featured offering emphasis
5. My Media and Testimonials section below the main content or in a secondary tab block
6. Customer portal access link

## 9A.4 Bio Tab Requirements
The Bio tab must display:
* profile biography text
* optional credentials/designations
* optional specialties (e.g., natal chart, relationship chart, tarot)
* optional languages
* optional experience summary
* optional mission/introduction content
* optional profile links/social links if permitted by admin

## 9A.5 Live Stream Module

### Live States
1. **Not Live** — display fallback content; hide Check-In form; optionally show next live time
2. **Live on One Platform** — embed that stream; show live badge; show Check-In CTA/form
3. **Live on Multiple Platforms** — show platform selection controls; allow switching

### Diviner Back-Office Config Needed
* platform enabled/disabled status
* stream URL or embed URL per platform
* optional stream key/reference metadata
* default fallback content when not live
* display order of platforms
* live status override

## 9A.6 Product and Service Listing

### Product Categories
1. **Time-Based Products** — Solar Return, Jupiter Return, Saturn Return, yearly transit readings
2. **Non-Time-Based Products** — Natal Chart, Relationship Chart, Tarot, compatibility reading

### Product Listing Requirements
Each product card must support: title, short description, category, price, delivery type, expected turnaround, featured badge, CTA button.

### Featured Product Rule
The Nativity/Birth Chart offering must be visually emphasized: featured badge, first position, highlighted card style, or dedicated callout section.

## 9A.7 Check-In Feature (Live Only)

### Visibility Rule
The Check-In CTA/form must appear only when the diviner is currently live.

### Data to Collect
* First Name, Last Name, Email, Birth Date, Birth City, Birth Time

### Check-In Submission Outcomes
1. save record against diviner and live session
2. create or link a lead/customer profile
3. mark consent preferences
4. enroll user into reminder logic if consented
5. optionally show follow-up CTA to purchase applicable reading

## 9A.8 My Media Section
Each media item should support: title, thumbnail, source platform, media URL/embed URL, category/tag, sort order, active/inactive state.

## 9A.9 Testimonials Section
* show approved testimonials only
* optionally highlight featured testimonials
* "Write me a testimonial" CTA
* testimonial submission through moderation workflow
* prevent spam and abuse

## 9A.10 Customer Login Link
A public CTA/link must be shown: "Access your personal divination back office" — routes to login or directly into customer portal if authenticated.

# 9B. Product Catalog and Service Model

## 9B.1 Product Types
1. One-time reading product
2. Scheduled/private session product
3. Recurring subscription product
4. Digital deliverable product
5. Free/lead capture offer (optional)

## 9B.2 Product Configuration Fields
product name, slug, owner diviner, category, type, short description, long description, price, compare-at price, active/inactive status, featured flag, intake form template, delivery method, expected turnaround, visibility scope, affiliate eligibility, targeted landing settings

## 9B.3 Dynamic Intake Form Requirement
Each product must define exactly what post-payment information is required.

### Example Configuration Matrix
* Natal Chart: full birth date, birth time, birth city
* Solar Return: birth details + target year or context
* Relationship Chart: user birth details + second person birth details
* Tarot: question + email, birth details optional
* Weekly Updates Subscription: minimum account/contact info only

# 9C. Purchase Flow

## 9C.1 Core Purchase Rule
**Payment must be collected before the detailed reading intake form is shown.**

## 9C.2 Purchase Flow Steps
1. User lands on diviner page or product page
2. User selects product
3. User sees product summary and price
4. User proceeds to checkout
5. Payment successfully authorized/captured
6. Order is created
7. Only then the system requests detailed product-specific intake data
8. User submits intake
9. Diviner receives order and required details
10. Customer can track or access order in their portal

## 9C.3 Guest vs Account Purchase
* guest checkout with post-purchase account creation prompt, OR
* mandatory lightweight account creation during checkout

## 9C.4 Checkout Requirements
product summary, diviner identity confirmation, applied affiliate/referral attribution, taxes if applicable, subscription terms when recurring, legal consent, success/failure handling

## 9C.5 Post-Purchase Intake Requirements
* save and continue later
* validation of required fields
* clear confirmation on submission
* order status update when intake is complete

## 9C.6 Fulfillment Statuses
pending_payment → paid → awaiting_intake → intake_submitted → in_progress → scheduled → delivered → cancelled / refunded

## 12. Business Rules and Validation Rules

### 12.1 Username Rules
* must be unique
* must meet format constraints
* must avoid reserved platform terms

### 12.2 Live Check-In Rules
* only visible when live status is true
* email required
* birth data validation required
* consent capture required if reminders/marketing are enabled

### 12.3 Product Purchase Rules
* inactive or hidden products cannot be purchased
* payment must complete before detailed intake is requested
* intake requirements are product-specific

### 12.4 Commission Rules
* effective commission rate must be snapshotted at time of conversion
* affiliate payout liability must remain traceable even if rates change later

### 12.5 Subscription Rules
* recurring products must support cancellation and status history
* weekly updates must stop after cancellation

### 12.6 Testimonial Rules
* testimonials are not public until approved
* public testimonial form must be protected from spam and malicious input

### 12.7 Telephony Rules
* pass-through charges based on measured usage
* charges appear in diviner billing history
* legal acceptance required before enabling dial-in feature

## 19. Final Product Summary

At completion, AstrologyPro should operate as a full diviner commerce and engagement platform where:

* every diviner has a branded public hub
* live audiences can convert into leads and buyers
* products are sold cleanly with payment-first logic
* required reading intake is dynamic and efficient
* customers can return through a dedicated portal
* affiliates can drive targeted sales with tracked commissions
* diviners can manage their storefront, audience, products, testimonials, media, and billing
* admins can oversee all operations, revenue rules, and platform growth

This requirement should be treated as the baseline product definition for design, database planning, API design, implementation tickets, QA cases, and release planning.
