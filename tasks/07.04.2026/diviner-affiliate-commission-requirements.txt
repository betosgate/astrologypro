Diviner Affiliate Commission Management — Detailed Requirement Analysis 1. Executive Summary This document defines the detailed requirements for an Affiliate Management and Commission Tracking module centered around a Diviner role . In this business model: - A Diviner can create and manage affiliates under their own network. - An Admin can also create and manage affiliates on behalf of a diviner. - Each affiliate belongs to a diviner. - Affiliates receive a commission on sales generated through their unique referral links. - Commission percentages or fixed values can be defined by the diviner or by admin. - Affiliates can receive product-targeted unique links . - The Diviner is responsible for paying out the affiliate , not the platform directly. - The system must provide full visibility into: - affiliate registrations - referral links - attributed orders - earned commissions - payout history - pending payable amounts - disputes / adjustments - audit logs This module should be treated as a financially sensitive operational system , not just a basic referral feature. 2. Business Objective The purpose of this module is to allow diviners to build, manage, incentivize, and track affiliate-driven sales in a controlled and auditable way. Main goals Allow diviners to onboard affiliates under them. Allow admin to create/manage affiliates on behalf of diviners. Allow each affiliate to have trackable unique links. Support product-specific referral campaigns. Accurately calculate affiliate commissions. Allow diviner to record manual payouts. Maintain a complete financial and operational audit trail. Provide dashboards for admin, diviner, and affiliate. Reduce disputes by having clear rules, statuses, and histories. 3. User Roles 3.1 Admin The highest privileged operational user. Admin capabilities create diviner accounts create affiliate accounts assign affiliate to a diviner reassign affiliate to another diviner if allowed by policy define or override commission settings create product referral links on behalf of diviner edit affiliate details suspend or activate affiliate review all sales and commissions review payout records manually adjust commission records when needed lock records after finance validation resolve disputes access audit logs configure global rules and policies 3.2 Diviner This is the primary business owner for a network of affiliates. Diviner capabilities invite or create affiliates under themselves view list of their affiliates set affiliate commission terms within admin-defined limits generate unique affiliate links generate product-specific referral links see all sales attributed to their affiliates see pending commission and payout due amounts record manual payouts made to affiliates upload payout proof if needed add notes against payouts or commission adjustments deactivate or block affiliate relationship subject to policy monitor performance metrics Diviner restrictions cannot view affiliates under other diviners cannot edit global system rules cannot modify locked financial records unless allowed by admin cannot override commission ceiling if admin sets limits 3.3 Affiliate A referral partner assigned under one diviner. Affiliate capabilities access their own affiliate dashboard view profile and assigned diviner see their unique referral links copy/share product-specific links view referred clicks, leads, orders, conversion metrics view commission earned view pending, approved, paid, and rejected commissions view payout history update limited profile information optionally upload tax/payment details Affiliate restrictions cannot set their own commission unless policy allows request workflow cannot view other affiliates cannot edit transaction or payout records cannot change diviner assignment 4. Core Business Rules 4.1 Affiliate Ownership Rule Every affiliate must belong to exactly one diviner at a time. The relationship must be represented explicitly in the database. Historical ownership changes must be preserved. 4.2 Commission Responsibility Rule Commission is earned by the affiliate. The diviner is responsible for paying the affiliate. The platform tracks payable and paid amounts but does not necessarily execute payment. 4.3 Attribution Rule A commissionable sale must be attributable to an affiliate through approved tracking logic. Possible attribution sources: - unique referral link click - referral code entered at checkout - tracked lead converted later - direct product-targeted campaign URL 4.4 Commission Rule Ownership Commission configuration can be set by: - admin - diviner - admin on behalf of diviner Priority should be clearly defined. Recommended order: 1. order-level manual override 2. affiliate-product-specific rule 3. affiliate default rule 4. diviner default rule 5. system default rule 4.5 Payout Rule Payouts are recorded after diviner pays affiliate externally. A payout record must reduce pending payable balance. One payout may cover multiple commission entries. Payout proof and notes should be optionally stored. 4.6 Immutable Financial History Rule Once a commission entry or payout entry is finalized or locked: - it should not be hard deleted - only reversal / adjustment entries should be allowed - full audit trail must be preserved 5. MVP Recommendation MVP must include admin, diviner, affiliate roles affiliate creation by diviner/admin assignment to diviner commission rules basic product-specific referral links order attribution commission ledger payout recording dashboards basic audit logs Phase 2 disputes partial payouts and advanced allocation UI campaign-level commissions downloadable statements approval workflows anomaly detection Phase 3 tier commissions advanced attribution windows self-serve affiliate onboarding workflow finance reconciliation automation wallet or integrated payout engine if desired later 19. Key Architect Recommendations Treat commissions as a ledger , not as a recalculated dashboard number. Never overwrite historical financial entries silently. Keep diviner-affiliate relationship history . Separate attribution logic , commission logic , and payout logic into different backend services/modules. Build strong status models from day one. Add full audit logging for every money-impacting action. Ensure refunds, reversals, and adjustments are supported early. Make admin override possible, but always with reason capture. 20. Final Conclusion This module is not just an affiliate link feature. It is a relationship management + sales attribution + commission ledger + payout tracking system . If built properly, it will allow: - diviners to build and manage affiliate networks - affiliates to promote separate products through unique links - admin to control and supervise the process - the business to maintain accurate and auditable records of commissions and payouts This is the correct requirement scope for a serious production-grade implementation. 21.
