Gold-Standard Job Ticket / Support Ticket Module 1. Module Purpose This module will serve as the central work management and support resolution system for the platform. It must support both: Support Tickets — issues, questions, complaints, requests, disputes, and service assistance raised by customers, diviners, affiliates, or internal users. Job Tickets — operational work items created for internal execution by support, finance, content, tech, moderation, fulfillment, or admin teams. The goal is not to create a simple helpdesk. The goal is to build a full operational ticketing backbone that can handle customer support, cross-team collaboration, approval flows, escalations, and internal execution with complete visibility, auditability, and SLA control. 2. Strategic Objectives The module must: Provide a single source of truth for all support and operational requests. Reduce resolution time with automation, routing, templates, and clear ownership. Allow different personas to raise and track issues without confusion. Let support teams collaborate with finance, tech, content, operations, and admin without losing context. Preserve a complete timeline and audit trail for every action. Support external requester communication and internal-only collaboration separately. Enable SLA management, escalation logic, and workload reporting. Support future growth into omnichannel support, AI assistance, and deeper workflow orchestration. 3. Product Philosophy This should be designed as a unified work item system with role-aware behavior, not two disconnected modules. 4.1 Unification Principle A support ticket and a job ticket should share a common core data structure: - requester - category - status - priority - owner - timeline - notes - attachments - related records - SLA / due dates But they should differ in: - intake channel - requester visibility - external communication requirements - approval rules - execution steps - completion criteria 4.2 Clean Separation of Communication The system must clearly separate: - Public conversation visible to requester - Internal notes visible only to authorized staff - System logs for audit / automation events 4.3 Operational Control Every ticket must always have: - current owner or queue - current state - next action expectation - SLA posture - last response timestamp - related business entities If any of these are missing, the ticket is operationally weak. 5. Primary Actors and Roles 5.1 External Roles Guest user (limited pre-login ticket entry for selected issues only) Registered customer Diviner Affiliate 5.2 Internal Roles Support Agent Support Lead / Support Manager Admin Finance Ops Content Ops Fulfillment / Order Ops Moderation Team Tech Support / Engineering Super Admin 5.3 Permission Philosophy Access must be based on both: - role-based permissions - record-level visibility rules Example: - Customer can only see own tickets. - Diviner can see tickets created by them and tickets related to their diviner account where platform policy allows access. - Affiliate can only see own affiliate tickets and payout-linked issues. - Support agents can see assigned queues only. - Finance can view payout/refund/billing queues. - Tech can view bug / integration / incident tickets but not private financial conversations unless explicitly granted. 6. Status Model The module should support a configurable state machine with sensible defaults. 7.1 Universal Statuses New Open Triaged Assigned In Progress Waiting on Requester Waiting on Internal Team Waiting on Third Party Pending Approval On Hold Resolved Closed Reopened Cancelled Merged Spam / Invalid 7.2 Job Ticket Specific Statuses Draft Submitted Under Review Approved Queued In Execution Blocked QA Review Completed Rejected 7.3 State Transition Rules The system must support admin-defined valid transitions. Example: - New -&gt; Triaged - Triaged -&gt; Assigned - Assigned -&gt; In Progress - In Progress -&gt; Waiting on Requester - In Progress -&gt; Pending Approval - Pending Approval -&gt; Approved / Rejected - Resolved -&gt; Closed - Closed -&gt; Reopened Invalid direct jumps should be blocked unless user has override rights. 7.4 Reopen Logic A closed/resolved ticket may reopen when: - requester replies within reopen window - agent reopens manually - automation detects unresolved linked failure Reopen count must be tracked for quality reporting. 8. Priority, Severity, and SLA Priority must not be a loose label only. It must drive queue ordering, SLA, notifications, and escalation. 8.1 Priority Levels P1 Critical P2 High P3 Medium P4 Low 8.2 Severity Dimensions Severity should be computed from: - business impact - user impact - revenue impact - brand risk - safety / abuse risk - technical spread (single user vs many users) 8.3 SLA Dimensions Each queue/category must support: - first response SLA - first meaningful action SLA - target resolution SLA - escalation threshold - customer follow-up threshold 8.4 Sample SLA Matrix Priority First Response Target Resolution Escalation P1 Critical 15 min 4 hours 30 min P2 High 1 hour 1 business day 4 hours P3 Medium 4 business hours 3 business days 1 business day P4 Low 1 business day 5 business days 3 business days 8.5 SLA Pause Rules SLA clocks may pause only in controlled statuses such as: - Waiting on Requester - Waiting on Third Party - Pending Approval Pause/resume must be fully auditable. 9. Ticket Intake Channels The platform should support multiple intake paths. 9.1 Manual UI-Based Intake Customer portal form Diviner dashboard form Affiliate dashboard form Internal staff quick-create form Admin creation on behalf of user 9.2 Triggered / Contextual Intake From order detail page: “Need help” From booking detail page: “Report issue” From course lesson page: “Can’t access / Report issue” From payout page: “Raise payout ticket” From live stream page: “Report stream/chat issue” From profile/verification page: “Request review” 9.3 System / Automated Intake Payment failure webhook creates ticket Repeated OTP failure creates ticket flag Failed course unlock automation creates investigation ticket Repeated stream failure creates incident ticket Abuse detection creates moderation ticket SLA breach creates escalation ticket 9.4 Email-to-Ticket Future-ready support for inbound email parsing: - unique support inbox per environment / queue - new email creates ticket - reply with thread token appends to ticket - unauthorized sender handling - spam filtering 10. Key Workflow Scenarios 12.1 Customer Refund Request Flow Customer raises refund ticket from order page. Order context auto-attached. Ticket auto-routed to billing/refund queue. System checks refund policy and order age. Agent reviews and sends acknowledgement. If approval required, job ticket sent to finance/admin. Decision recorded. Public response sent. If approved, refund reference linked. Ticket resolved and CSAT triggered. 12.2 Diviner Live Stream Issue Flow Diviner clicks “Report live issue.” Stream/session/platform context auto-linked. Ticket marked high priority during live window. Routed to live operations / tech support queue. Support adds internal notes and workaround. If platform outage suspected, incident ticket created. Resolution communicated to diviner. Ticket closed after stream end and confirmation. 12.3 Affiliate Commission Dispute Flow Affiliate raises issue from commission page. Referral link and order context auto-attached. Ticket routed to affiliate finance queue. System surfaces commission calculation logs. Reviewer validates attribution. If data inconsistency found, child job ticket to tech/data ops. Final adjustment decision logged. Affiliate notified. 12.4 Bug Report to Engineering Flow User or support flags technical bug. Support triages and confirms reproducibility. Ticket converted or linked to internal bug ticket. Severity and affected modules recorded. Engineering owner assigned. Fix status updated through internal notes or integration. Parent support tickets synced with high-level status. Once fixed, public communication goes out. Related tickets can be bulk resolved if applicable. 12.5 Abuse / Safety Report Flow User submits complaint or report. Safety category triggers restricted visibility. Only moderation/admin roles can view sensitive details. Evidence retained securely. Optional immediate action workflow available. Outcome logged with policy reason. External requester sees limited safe response. 13. Functional Requirements FR-1 Ticket Creation The system must allow authorized roles to create tickets manually or through contextual pages. FR-2 Dynamic Forms Form fields must adapt based on ticket type, category, requester role, and linked context. FR-3 Auto-Assignment Tickets must support routing by category, subcategory, requester role, business object, language, priority, and time window. FR-4 Ownership Each ticket must support: - queue ownership - primary assignee - secondary collaborators - watchers / followers FR-5 Public and Internal Communication The system must allow separate public replies and internal notes in the same ticket without leakage. FR-6 Attachments Support upload, preview, download, virus scan workflow, and attachment tagging. FR-7 Related Records A ticket must be linkable to one or more business objects: - user - diviner - affiliate - order - order item - payment - payout batch - course - lesson - booking - session - livestream - product - refund record - invoice - incident FR-8 Activity Timeline Every user action and system action must appear in a unified timeline with actor, timestamp, action type, and visibility. FR-9 Status Rules Status changes must follow configured transition rules, with optional mandatory note/reason enforcement. FR-10 SLA Engine The platform must compute and track SLA clocks and breach risks in near real time. FR-11 Escalation Tickets must support manual and automated escalation to another queue, team, or approval flow. FR-12 Approvals Some tickets must require one or more approvers before completion. FR-13 Job Subtasks / Checklists Internal tickets must support structured task checklists and assignee-level subtasks. FR-14 Merge and Split The system must support duplicate merge and child-ticket split workflows while retaining lineage. FR-15 Template / Macro Support Internal users must be able to use approved canned replies, note templates, and checklists. FR-16 Search and Filtering The system must support fast full-text search plus structured filters across all major ticket dimensions. FR-17 Notifications The module must support in-app, email, and future-ready push / messaging notifications. FR-18 Reopen and Auto-Close Tickets may reopen based on policy. Resolved tickets may auto-close after configurable no-response period. FR-19 Satisfaction Survey After resolution, external users may rate support and leave feedback. FR-20 Reporting The module must provide operational and strategic reports for admins and managers. FR-21 Audit Logging Every sensitive or state-changing action must be auditable. FR-22 Data Retention and Privacy Attachments, notes, and conversations must follow retention policy and role-based access restrictions. 14. Advanced Features Required for Gold Standard 14.1 Smart Duplicate Detection When new ticket is created, system should check: - same requester - same related entity - similar subject/body - recent open tickets Then suggest: - join existing ticket - continue previous conversation - create new anyway 14.2 Suggested Knowledge Articles Before submit and during agent reply, system should show likely help articles or SOPs. 14.3 AI-Assisted Triage Future-ready support for: - category suggestion - priority suggestion - duplicate suggestion - macro suggestion - summary generation - sentiment and risk detection 14.4 Parent-Child Ticketing One major issue may generate multiple execution tickets across departments. Example: Parent ticket = “Customer payout dispute” Child tickets: - finance verification - tech log validation - support follow-up 14.5 Approval Chains Refunds, manual payouts, moderation decisions, and sensitive actions may need one-step or multi-step approvals. 14.6 Internal Playbooks Category-based checklist templates should auto-load. Example: - refund investigation checklist - affiliate commission dispute checklist - stream outage checklist - abusive user review checklist 14.7 Business Impact Flagging Ticket can be flagged for: - VIP requester - high lifetime value customer - active live event impact - many affected users - reputation risk - legal/policy sensitivity 14.8 Cross-Ticket Incident Linking If many tickets come from one root problem, they should link to a single incident for management visibility. 15. Acceptance Criteria The module should be considered production-ready only when: External users can raise and track tickets without confusion. Internal teams can triage, assign, collaborate, and resolve tickets from one workspace. No internal note can accidentally become public. All high-priority tickets are visible with SLA posture. Tickets can be linked to the business records that caused them. Finance / tech / content / moderation escalations are supported cleanly. Reporting can identify major pain points and operational bottlenecks. Audit trail is complete enough for dispute and performance review. Queue ownership is always clear. Repeated issues can be identified and managed at root-cause level. 28. Architect Recommendation For this platform, the best approach is one unified ticketing domain with support-first UX and job-ticket extensions , not two separate disconnected systems. That gives you: - less duplicated logic - better auditability - shared search/reporting - easier escalation chains - better operational visibility - simpler long-term maintenance The final result should feel like a combination of: - customer helpdesk - internal ops work tracker - escalation engine - approval system - incident management layer That is the right gold-standard direction for a serious platform. 29.
