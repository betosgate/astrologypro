Detailed Requirement Analysis Divine / AstrologyPro Platform Derived from the uploaded April 3, 2026 product discussion recording What this document does Converts the meeting discussion into a structured product requirement document suitable for architecture, UI/API planning, and sprint breakdown. Separates the platform into four product layers: Diviner SaaS, Certification School, Community Membership, and Mystery School / Ritual Technology. Marks business numbers and policy amounts as working assumptions when they sounded provisional in the recording. Prepared for implementation planning 1. Executive Summary The platform being discussed is not one single feature set. It is a connected product ecosystem with four distinct but related layers: (1) AstrologyPro as a business platform for diviners, astrologers, tarot readers, and oracle practitioners; (2) a certification training system for becoming a certified diviner; (3) a community membership product for astrology-interested individuals and families; and (4) a deeper Mystery School path supported by ritual technology. A critical business rule is that platform usage must not depend on certification. A practitioner must be able to subscribe to AstrologyPro and run a business on the platform without graduating from the school. Certification adds benefits such as a badge and search prioritization, but it must not be a gate for using the SaaS itself. The recording also establishes concrete expectations around scheduling, billing, refunds, affiliate handling, live-streaming, newsletter automation, back-office content delivery, and the sequencing of work. The full Mystery School experience can be phased, but the ritual technology itself is a critical path dependency because it must be ready before the founder begins the school cycle in fall. Product principle carried through the entire meeting Do not force people to become graduates before they can use the software. Use certification as a trust signal and ranking advantage, not as an access lock. Keep the system commercially practical first; deeper school features come after the core business flows are solid. 2. Business Goals and Non-Goals 3.1 Business Goals Allow any legitimate practitioner to subscribe and operate on AstrologyPro immediately. Use certification to increase trust, conversion, and search visibility. Create a consumer astrology community that naturally feeds paid reading demand into practitioners. Provide clear monetization through commissions, memberships, training fees, and advanced paths. Support creator-style growth through newsletters, live-streaming, and practitioner-owned public URLs. 3.2 Explicit Non-Goals / Delayed Goals Do not make full social-community functionality a phase-1 requirement; richer social/community interactions are intentionally delayed. Do not require deep custom newsletter logic per practitioner when templated personalized monthly content can achieve the business goal. Do not ship a Mystery School back-office experience before the core SaaS, training, and community flows are stable. Do not create manual refund decision chaos; refund/no-show outcomes must be policy-driven and automated wherever possible. 4. Identity, Access, and Cross-System Experience The meeting opens with a requirement to keep the existing Divine training environment and let users move into the new system with minimal friction. The desired experience is effectively one-click continuation: a user who is already inside Divine should be able to enter or activate AstrologyPro without a disjointed second onboarding flow. 5.1 Functional Requirements Support a unified identity across Divine and AstrologyPro, or a tightly connected account-linking flow that feels like one-click activation. Separate authentication from entitlement. A user may share one identity across systems while having different subscription states in training, community, AstrologyPro, and Mystery School. Support role coexistence. The same human may be a community member, a student, a diviner, and later a certified diviner. Access control must be entitlement-based rather than path-based. Example: a student can begin using AstrologyPro even before graduation. Public profile URLs for diviners must be available even when the user never completed certification. 5.2 Derived Technical Requirements Single sign-on or shared session design should be prioritized. Central user profile should hold core identity while product-specific modules hold separate settings and entitlements. Every user-visible page should render the correct state based on current subscriptions, not on assumptions about the path they followed. 6. AstrologyPro SaaS Requirements AstrologyPro is the commercial operating system for practitioners. It must stand on its own. The product must clearly market itself as the place where a practitioner can run the business side of astrology/tarot/oracle services, not merely as an add-on for school graduates. 6.1 Marketing and Public Positioning Marketing copy must explain why a practitioner should use this system instead of generic tools such as Zoom plus separate astrology software. Messaging should emphasize that the platform is dialogue-based and built for diviner-client interaction, sharing, charts, and consultation flow. The public website must use clean screenshots and updated product copy; stale or broken screenshots are not acceptable. A subtle in-app banner may promote certification and the badge, but it must not shame or pressure non-certified users. 6.2 Public Diviner Profile / Personal URL Each diviner must receive or configure a public profile URL (for example, /username). The public page must support biography, specialties, service list, pricing, booking entry points, policy links, newsletter capture, and live/embed areas. The public page must be valid enough for processor verification, meaning policy content and required business information should be available on that page or clearly linked from it. Diviners should be encouraged during live sessions to send viewers to their own URL rather than leaving the audience only on Facebook/YouTube. 6.3 Search and Marketplace Discovery Provide a 'Find a Diviner' search/browse experience. Certified graduates must rank above non-certified diviners when applicable. Search filters should include at minimum role/sub-type: astrologer, tarot reader, and oracle (both). A non-certified practitioner must still be fully searchable and bookable. 7. Certification School Requirements The certification system is a structured training product that produces officially certified diviners. Certification ends with a counselor interaction and should then push graduates toward using AstrologyPro commercially. 7.1 Training Structure Training is expected to be extensive. The recording estimates roughly 60 trainings, potentially hundreds of lessons/tests. Each lesson may contain slides, videos, audio intros, and related media assets. Tarot content requires a higher level of manual curation than generic AI-produced slides; the system must support frequent content revision. Certification training should feel like guided progression, not a loose content library. 7.2 Learning Flow Students progress through lesson content and answer knowledge checks as they go. Quizzes are intended to be multiple-choice for simplicity and scale. A practical default is two multiple-choice questions per slide/section checkpoint where appropriate. If a learner misses a section, the system should require them to rewatch/review and answer correctly before advancing. Completion of the full path triggers graduation state and immediately opens counselor scheduling. 7.3 Counselor / Graduation Stage After completing the training path, the learner must schedule a counselor session. If the learner does not schedule the counselor session, the system must continue reminder emails until the step is completed. The counselor stage serves both quality assurance and commercial conversion into AstrologyPro usage. Counselor meetings should be schedulable through the system rather than manually coordinated off-platform. 7.4 Certification Outcomes Graduated users receive certified-diviner status. Certified-diviner status must grant a visible badge. Certified-diviner status must increase search priority on the marketplace. Certification status must not revoke the ability of non-graduates to use AstrologyPro. 8. Community Membership Requirements The community is a separate membership product for people interested in astrology, family dynamics, and chart interpretation, whether or not they want to become practitioners. 8.1 Membership Plans Support an individual monthly plan and a family monthly plan. Working assumptions from the meeting: individual community plan around $9.97/month; family plan around $19.97/month for up to five people. These values should remain configurable. Community pricing should stay intentionally affordable. 8.2 Member Data and Family Structure Community membership must support individuals, partners, children, and family dynamics. The system must allow storing birth data for multiple family members. Unknown birth time must not block sign-up; instead the system should prompt later for better accuracy. Users must be able to correct mistaken birth dates and related input data. 8.3 Community Deliverables Generate natal readings/charts for members. Generate relationship charts between relevant member combinations in the household/group. Age-sensitive chart interpretation rules must be supported; younger children may see a more limited interpretation, while more complete interpretation unlocks at older ages. For members above the configured adult threshold discussed in the meeting (working assumption: age 14+), the system must generate monthly transits. Members should be able to log in and view their charts and monthly transit interpretations from the back office. 8.4 Community-to-Marketplace Conversion Community members must see a clear upsell path to book a professional reader. The system should present a call to action such as getting a deeper interpretation from a professional astrologer. Community members receive a discount on practitioner services. Working business rule from the meeting: a 5% community discount is funded from the platform's commission share, not from the practitioner's payout. 9. Scheduling, Sessions, Pricing, Billing, and Refunds The recording contains unusually specific operational rules around booking and payments. These are not minor details; they are core trust and marketplace-protection rules and should be implemented as system-enforced policies. 9.1 Booking and Product Linkage Every appointment must be tied to a product/service. A product must define base duration, price, and overtime policy. The booking flow must initiate the connected payment setup for the practitioner. Freelance / general consultation products must be supported for users who do not yet know exactly which service they need. 9.2 Time-Based Billing During video sessions, the UI should visibly show elapsed billable time. Products may have different default durations such as 30 minutes or 60 minutes. Working rule from the meeting: overtime is billed at $0.50 per minute past the purchased duration. Make this configurable globally or per product family. 9.3 Return / Milestone Products Return products such as Jupiter return, Saturn return, and Solar return should not be positioned as generic front-door products for most users. These should primarily be triggered after the user becomes a customer or newsletter subscriber and the system knows their relevant dates. The platform must support dedicated single-product landing pages for these event-based products. 9.4 Refund and No-Show Policies If the practitioner fails to attend, the system must issue an automatic full refund. Working rule from the meeting: if the practitioner has not entered the room within five minutes of the scheduled start time, the system treats it as practitioner no-show. If the client fails to attend, the system should apply the configured no-show policy automatically rather than leaving it to individual practitioner discretion. Working rule from the meeting: client no-show may result in a 50% refund / 50% retained booking fee. This must be configurable but policy-driven. Checkout and policy pages must clearly explain these rules before purchase. 9.5 Payment Processor Connectivity Practitioners must be able to connect their own payout processor account. Stripe support already exists conceptually and is mandatory. PayPal support is a stated desired capability and should be explored as a first-class option, not a manual workaround. The connection flow must be low-friction. The practitioner should connect Stripe or PayPal with minimal steps. 9.6 Settlement Model The system should retain a card on file or equivalent transaction authority. The meeting suggests post-event charging/settlement after the session is confirmed to have occurred, rather than blindly capturing money before outcome verification. Attendance should be detectable from room/session participation so billing can reflect what actually happened. 10. Affiliate Requirements Affiliates exist under practitioners, not as a centrally paid marketplace partner model. A diviner must be able to enable or disable affiliate functionality. A diviner must be able to set an affiliate commission percentage. Working example from the meeting: 5%. The system must generate affiliate links and attribute conversions to the correct affiliate/diviner pair. The platform calculates commission amounts but does not pay affiliates directly. The diviner is responsible for affiliate payouts. Before enabling affiliate mode, the diviner must sign/accept a policy stating responsibility for paying affiliates. Admin should be able to audit affiliate-generated bookings and commission calculations. 11. Badge, Ranking, and Credibility Rules Certification yields a visible Divine-certified badge. Badge placement should appear on search results and practitioner profile pages. Certified practitioners should receive ranking priority in search. The promotional banner for certification inside the SaaS should be subtle and aspirational, not punitive. Public and internal labeling must distinguish between active practitioner status and certified status. 12. Live Streaming, Calendar, and Audience Capture The live-stream flow is not a side feature. It is positioned as a growth channel for diviners who go live on platforms such as YouTube and Facebook while driving viewers back to their owned AstrologyPro URL. 12.1 Core Live Requirements A diviner must be able to indicate that they stream on Facebook, YouTube, or both. Their homepage/profile should support embedding or presenting the live content area elegantly. The live area must look good both when live content exists and when it does not. The platform should allow a diviner to create a recurring live schedule. 12.2 Calendar Interaction Live schedule blocks must automatically make the corresponding booking slots unavailable. The booking engine must prevent customers from booking readings during scheduled live windows. Timezone handling must be explicit and stored per diviner. 12.3 Audience Conversion UX Back-office guidance should teach diviners how to use live sessions to drive viewers to their own URL. Live-related pages should encourage actions such as watch, subscribe, book, and join newsletter. The system should support live notifications/subscription concepts beyond third-party platform bells where practical. 13. Notification and Marketing Automation The recording defines two main automation patterns: broad monthly astrology/newsletter content and highly specific event-driven reminders based on known customer birth data. 13.1 Newsletter Sign-up Newsletter sign-up must work on public-facing pages. Newsletter records should be attributable to the specific diviner profile under which the user subscribed. The newsletter may be templated centrally but branded as though it comes from the selected practitioner. 13.2 Monthly Content Send a branded monthly mundane-astrology newsletter. The base content can be shared globally, but the sender identity, branding, and call to action should align to the individual diviner. Newsletter content should include links back to the diviner's public page and calls to book readings. 13.3 Event-Triggered Lifecycle Messaging When customer birth data enables important date prediction, the system should send milestone reminders such as month before, week before, day before, and day of. Event examples explicitly mentioned: Jupiter return, Saturn return, and Solar return. Email cadence can be richer than SMS. SMS should be used sparingly. The event system should work for customers and possibly for newsletter subscribers when enough data is known. 14. Back Office Content and Resource Library Community back office should include charts, transit access, and educational resources. Back office should surface big-button links to existing central doctrine content on the website and YouTube. Back office should include resource links such as Five-Fold Creed and Astrological Theurgy introductory content. Provide free PDF access to the identified book resources in the community context where legally appropriate. Support recurring founder content drops such as Sunday Service and weekly topic videos as content assets in the back office. 15. Mystery School and Ritual Technology This is the most specialized layer and should be treated separately from general community and certification. However, one part of it is urgent: the ritual technology itself must be completed before the founder begins the cycle in fall. 15.1 Enrollment and Pricing Mystery School entry fee and monthly subscription should be configurable. Working assumptions from the recording: one-time entry fee around $97 and monthly price corrected to roughly $27/month replacing the lower community membership price. Mystery School membership replaces community pricing rather than stacking the lower community fee on top of it. 15.2 Learning Sequence Students choose a starting seasonal quarter (equinox or solstice based entry logic). The first stage is a defined 12-week preparation period. The system must support slide-based/audio-assisted instruction for the 12-week path. After the initial stage, the student enters deacon-based ritual cycles. 15.3 Ritual Cycle Rules A deacon cycle is fixed content, not free ritual design. The ritual is predefined for the student. The system must open the relevant deacon set on schedule, with anticipation and gated access. One week before the relevant entry point, the full three-deacon set for that sign/sequence opens. Each deacon view must prominently display the exact date range during which the ritual must be performed. Students may perform the ritual along with the provided video playback or independently. 15.4 Required Student Actions Within each 10-day deacon period, the student must complete the ritual at least once. The student must perform associated scrying and journaling. A grace-period rule was discussed for missed windows; the final value should be configurable. The system must track completion state by deacon cycle. 15.5 Ritual Technology Capabilities Support video-driven ritual walkthroughs where the student can hit play and follow along. Support audio journal / commentary assets tied to deacon cycles. Support invocation segments and pause/resume moments where the user completes a step before continuing. After graduation, advanced users may gain access to ritual-builder capabilities for creating ritual sets. 16. Admin, Documentation, and QA Requirements An internal instructions / implementation page exists or is expected; it must be cleaned up and kept accurate. Screenshots used in documentation must be regenerated from a correct seeded state. The platform must support proper testing access for internal staff so the product can be validated from all angles. Broken or incomplete sign-up forms, bad field mapping, and stale assets must be treated as release blockers. Admin must be able to manage policies, pricing, memberships, content links, ranking logic, and notification templates. 17. Key End-to-End Workflows A. Practitioner SaaS onboarding Visitor lands on AstrologyPro marketing site. Practitioner subscribes without needing to be a graduate. Practitioner sets sub-type, public URL, pricing, availability, and payment processor. Practitioner optionally enables affiliate mode and accepts affiliate-payout responsibility policy. Practitioner becomes searchable and bookable immediately. B. Student certification flow Student enters training system. Student consumes lesson content and completes progressive multiple-choice quizzes. If a section is failed, system requires rewatch/retry. Upon completion, the system opens counselor booking. After counselor completion, student becomes certified and receives badge/search advantage. C. Community member flow User buys individual or family membership. System collects birth data for self and family members. System generates natal and relationship charts. System delivers monthly transits for eligible ages. Member upgrades into a paid reader booking with member discount. D. Session booking and refund flow Client selects a product from a practitioner profile. Booking reserves the time slot and records policy acceptance. Session attendance is tracked from room participation. If practitioner no-shows beyond threshold, full refund is triggered automatically. If client no-shows, configured retention/refund rule is applied automatically. E. Event-driven remarketing flow User becomes customer or newsletter subscriber. System stores birth data and computes important future events. System sends timed reminders and routes the user to dedicated landing pages. User purchases the targeted event reading from the associated practitioner. F. Mystery School ritual flow Student enrolls and completes 12-week preparation. Relevant deacon set opens with date-bound access. Student plays ritual guidance, performs ritual, scries, and journals. System records deacon completion and progress toward graduation. Advanced ritual-builder access is granted after qualifying progression/graduation. 18. Architecture Takeaway The strongest architectural decision from the meeting is this: model the platform as one identity system with multiple monetized modules, not as one rigid funnel. A user should be able to enter from community, school, or practitioner SaaS and still be routed into the other layers over time. That is the cleanest way to preserve the agreed business rules, reduce UX friction, and avoid a fragmented technical architecture.
